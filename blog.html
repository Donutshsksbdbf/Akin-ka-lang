Viruses A computer virus is a type of malicious software that attaches itself to legitimate files or programs, replicates itself, and spreads across systems. Characteristics: Requires a host file or program to operate. Spreads through email attachments, downloads, or infected devices. Can corrupt files, slow down systems, and even render devices inoperable.

Examples:

ILOVEYOU Virus (2000): Spread via email, causing billions of dollars in damages.

Conficker (2008): A worm that infected millions of systems worldwide.
Malware

Malware is a broader term that includes all types of malicious software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise systems and data.

Types of Malware:

Trojan Horse: Disguises itself as legitimate software to deceive users.

Ransomware: Encrypts data and demands payment for its release (e.g., WannaCry, 2017).

Spyware: Secretly collects user information (e.g., passwords, browsing habits).

Adware: Delivers unwanted advertisements, often as pop-ups.

Worms: Self-replicating programs that spread across networks without user intervention.

Effects of Malware:

Theft of sensitive information. Financial losses. System downtime and data corruption.
Spam

Spam refers to unsolicited messages, often sent in bulk, typically for advertising or phishing purposes.

Characteristics:

Commonly delivered via email, social media, or messaging platforms. Often contains malicious links or attachments. May lead to identity theft or financial fraud.

Examples of Spam Threats:

Phishing Emails: Messages pretending to be from trusted organizations to steal login credentials.

Scam Offers: Fake lotteries or investment schemes.
Antiviruses

Antiviruses are software programs designed to detect, prevent, and remove viruses and other malicious software.

Functions of Antiviruses:

Real-Time Scanning: Monitors files and activities for suspicious behavior.

Heuristic Analysis: Detects previously unknown threats based on behavior patterns.

Signature-Based Detection: Compares files against a database of known malware.

Quarantine and Removal: Isolates infected files and removes malicious code.

Popular Antivirus Software:

Norton Antivirus

McAfee

Kaspersky

Avast

Windows Defender
Key Measures to Stay Protected

1. Install Antivirus Software: Regular updates ensure protection against emerging threats.

2. Avoid Suspicious Links: Do not click on unknown links or download attachments from untrusted sources.

3. Use Firewalls: Adds an additional layer of protection against unauthorized access.

4. Regular Updates: Keep operating systems and applications updated to patch vulnerabilities.

5. Strong Passwords: Use complex, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication.
